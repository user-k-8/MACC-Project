33% of girls do not return to school after falling pregnant. The lack of education diminishes opportunities for advancement available to teen mothers, perpetuating the inequality and poverty that made them vulnerable in the first place.

Our Story

The Founder, Thandoluhle Vilakazi speaks...

Thandoluhle fell pregnant in 2017 at the age of 20 while being a student in University. She took note of the continuous tremendous support she received from friends and family throughout her pregnancy and decided she wanted to grant other young mothers the opportunity to receive support and be a voice for those who have been silenced.

She envisions a future where young mothers are provided with holistic support systems that address their spiritual, physical, emotional, and psychological well-being. A society that values and invests in the potential of every young mother, offering mentorship, vocational training, and entrepreneurship programs to foster economic independence. By cultivating their leadership abilities and self-confidence, her mandate is to empower young mothers and young women to become agents of change, influencing policy and advocating for the rights of young parents everywhere
